An eligibility list may be established to fill similar regular and term positions, which will remain in effect for 12 months.  This competition may also be used to source candidates for Policy and Program Analysts at the PM1 classification. 

The Government of Manitoba is currently seeking qualified professionals to fill multiple regular and term Policy & Program Analysts (PM2) positions throughout the various Provincial Government Departments. Some Policy & Program Analyst positions are designated as bilingual (French/English), for these positions candidates must be able to read, write and verbally communicate in both official languages.

Policy & Program Analysts at the PM2 level are involved at all stages of the policy and program development cycle including research, analysis, consultation, developing recommendations and evaluation of Manitoba government programs and policies.  In addition, Policy & Program Analysts may provide centralized corporate support to their Department, manage special projects and initiatives, maintain responsibilities for strategic planning and corporate business processes, oversee communications, support public correspondence, and manage information systems.

Qualifications:
Essential:

  • Post-secondary Degree or Diploma in Public or Business Administration, Social Sciences, Education, Law, Economics, Environmental Studies, or a related discipline.  An equivalent combination of education, training and work experience may also be considered.
  • Experience conducting research and analysis on legislation, regulations, policies and/or programs to make fact-based assessments or recommendations preferably within a work setting. 
  • Experience interpreting and applying policy, regulations and/or legislation
  • Excellent written communication skills with experience writing a variety of documents and reports (such as reports, memos, letters) for different audiences.
  • Strong verbal communication skills to effectively communicate with various stakeholders.
  • Strong organizational and time management skills with the ability to manage multiple priorities with competing deadlines.
  • Strong interpersonal skills to develop and maintain strong working relationships with various stakeholders.
  • Ability to navigate sensitive issues with tact and diplomacy.
  • Ability to work independently with minimal supervision.
  • Experience working on projects preferably within a work setting, and preferably with knowledge of project management principles. 
  • Knowledge of, and sensitivity to, Indigenous issues to build relationships with Indigenous communities and organizations.
  • Ability to read, write and verbally communicate in French. (required for some positions)

Duties:
Reporting to their assigned Manager, Coordinator or Supervisor, the Policy & Program Analyst (PM2) will be responsible to research, review, evaluate and advise the applicable department on the feasibility of major programs and policy, and ensures consistency of programs with intended policy.

Other key duties may include: contributing to the planning and execution of the Department’s strategic initiatives; drafting various documents for departmental senior management or senior analysts; participating in project management and strategic planning to assist in designing and developing research projects/studies; developing methodologies in the preparation of project budgets; participating in intra/interdepartmental project teams, committees and working groups; undertaking research to assist in planning, developing, implementing, monitoring and evaluating legislation, policies and initiatives; suggesting policy and program alternatives, recommendations and methods to achieve established objectives; consulting with external organizations to share information concerning programs in relation to government policy; and providing support to stakeholders, management, staff and branches by undertaking evidence-based research, analysis and evaluations.
