The Financial and Administrative Services Branch (FASB) provides a broad range of financial and administrative support services to the various divisions within the Department of Families. The Accounting Services team within the Financial and Administrative Services Branch is responsible for processing financial transactions for the department. The manager is required to work independently in a team environment, demonstrate flexibility and effectiveness with changing environments, tasks, responsibilities and actively seeks new processes that provide service more effectively or efficiently.

Qualifications:
Essential:

  • Post-secondary education in Finance, Accounting related field, or equivalent combination of education and senior-level financial experience may be considered.
  • Experience applying the General Manual of Administration (GMA), Financial Administration Manual (FAM),  and Delegation of Financial Signing Authority (DFSA). 
  • Experience interpreting and applying financial and administrative policies, and internal controls. 
  • In depth experience with SAP financial management module with respect to all accounting transactions, procurement, and reporting.
  • Experience supervising and leading staff in a transformation focused environment. 
  • Strong analytical skills to research and translate problems and proposals into advice and recommendations for staff and senior management.
  • Strong verbal communication skills.
  • Strong written communication skills.
  • Strong interpersonal skills with the ability to interact with a wide range internal and external stakeholders.
  • Strong organizational and time management skills with the ability to prioritize and delegate work in a deadline driven environment.
Desired:
  • Ability to read, write and verbally communicate in French.

Duties:
The Manager of Accounting Services is accountable to support senior management both in the Administration & Finance division and in various program areas in carrying out functions associated with accounting transactions including commitment, accrual and cost accounting, reconciliations; and treasury duties. The incumbent provides interpretation, advice, and recommendations on expenditure, and liability controls. The incumbent review reconciliations on financial control accounts to ensure the accurate reporting and control of expenditures and liabilities. The incumbent also provide leadership to the Accounting Services unit within the Financial and Administrative Services Branch.
