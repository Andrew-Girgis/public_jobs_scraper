Help Shape Ontario's Global Economic Future

We're hiring Senior Economic Officers (SEOs) to represent Ontario in key international markets: London, Munich, Paris, and Tokyo. In these high-impact roles, you'll promote Ontario's trade and investment interests, build strategic partnerships, and provide expert advice on complex economic issues.

If you have a strong understanding of global markets and a passion for international business, this is your opportunity to make a real difference..

To access the job postings, please see the links in the opportunities section

Why Join Us:

• Competitive compensation and benefits.
• Career growth and development opportunities across multiple business areas.
• On-the-job training to support your success in the role.

About the job

As a Senior Economic Officer, you'll work with provincial, federal, and international partners to position Ontario as a top destination for investment and trade. You'll lead strategic initiatives, support trade missions, host delegations, and raise Ontario's profile through media and outreach. Your insights will help shape policy and drive economic growth across borders.

About the Team

You'll be part of the International Representation Branch within the Trade and International Engagement Division — a dynamic team that connects Ontario to the world.

With offices in Canadian embassies and consulates across Europe, Asia, and the Americas, our Trade and Investment Offices are led by experts who understand the unique needs of their markets — and the competitive advantages Ontario offers. We're also a key resource for local economic development teams looking to attract foreign investment or expand into global markets.

All external applicants (including former employees of the Ontario Public Service) applying to a competition in a ministry or Commission public body must disclose (either in the cover letter or resume) previous employment with the Ontario Public Service. Disclosure must include positions held, dates of employment and any active restrictions as applicable from being rehired by the Ontario Public Service. Active restrictions can include time and/or ministry-specific restrictions currently in force, and may preclude a former employee from being offered a position with the Ontario Public Service for a specific time period (e.g. one year), or from being offered a position with a specific ministry (either for a pre-determined time period or indefinitely). The circumstances around an employee's exit will be considered prior to an offer of employment.
