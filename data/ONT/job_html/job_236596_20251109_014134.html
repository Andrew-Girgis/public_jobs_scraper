About Us:

The Ontario Provincial Police (OPP) is one of North America's largest deployed police services with more than 5,800 uniformed officers, 2,600 civilian employees and 600 Auxiliary officers. We provide essential services that ensure the safety and security of the people of the Province of Ontario. The OPP is a division of the Ministry of the Solicitor General, the largest operational ministry in the province with a presence in every community across Ontario. We are the largest police service in Ontario and the second largest in Canada.

Business Management Bureau (BMB) holds itself accountable to providing high quality service to our clients and partners while managing critical business functions for the organization. BMB impacts every aspect of the OPP's operations. BMB considers the factors of timeliness, proficiency, extra mile, fairness, and outcome, when measuring the quality of our service delivery.

The OPP Facilities Section manages over 500 sites, supporting facility maintenance, new developments, lease management, and employee housing coordination. It also addresses infrastructure-related issues and long- and short-term accommodation needs across the organization.

BMB has worked hard to create a responsive, efficient and effective structure. We provide the organization and the people of Ontario with expertise and excellence that supports the frontline and enhances service delivery. We look forward to providing continued service excellence to all of our clients and partners.

About the job

In this role, you will:

• Plan and manage construction, accommodation, and leasing projects for OPP clients across Ontario.
• Provide technical advice and costing to ensure compliance with standards and contracts.
• Ensure adherence to accommodation requirements, coordinate facilities planning and construction projects.
• Provide technical advice to clients.
• Access, manipulate and evaluate data and information, prepare documentation and track and monitor projects using computer software.
• Act as the primary liaison between OPP clients and representatives of Infrastructure Ontario (IO) and its service providers.
• Participates in the resolution of contentious issues, serious occurrences and service related complaints from clients.

Note: The successful candidate will be expected to travel frequently throughout the province.

What you bring to the team


Mandatory requirements

• Ability to pass an OPP background/ security investigation.
• Must have a valid Ontario driver's licence.

Facilities Management Experience

• Knowledge of facilities management policies, procedures, guidelines and standards.
• Knowledge of accommodation and lease management processes, and business plans.
• Knowledge of Workplace Environment Guidelines (WEG), financial controllership, budget estimates and input into capital and strategic plans.
• Knowledge of architectural design, relevant construction codes (Building Code, Fire Code, etc.), legislation, including health and safety regulations.
• Knowledge of space planning, ergonomics, barrier-free (AODA) requirements and security construction (CPRED) and inspection practices.
• Ability to read architectural and engineering drawings to advise professionals and support project completion.

Project Management

• Ability to apply expertise in facilities and construction related project management to manage the delivery of facilities related projects.
• Knowledge of project management standards to lead and coordinate large-scale accommodation and leasing projects.

Analytical Skills

• Ability to apply problem solving and analytical skills to identify contentious issues and develop resolution strategies.
• Knowledge of facilities design, building sciences, and database systems to interpret technical data, monitor projects, and produce reports.
• Proficient in software and systems including AutoCAD, spreadsheets, word processing, project management, and records tracking and retention tools.

Communication and Relationship Management

• Proven ability to negotiate and resolve project and leasing issues with OPP clients, IO staff, and service providers.
• Ability to brief senior management on the status and resolution of contentious issues, serious occurrences and complaints from clients.
• Skill to communicate technical advice and information to management, staff and clients
• Ability to provide detailed technical instructions to service providers.
